Behind The Scenes

Explore production notes, set photos, interviews, and making-of features from Strange Harvest.

Behind the scenes of Strange Harvest, the process resembled building an investigative archive more than producing a conventional feature. From the outset, the film was designed to be modular in nature — constructed in individual segments that could function independently before locking together into a larger narrative structure. Interviews, archival broadcasts, reenactments, and investigative threads were developed as standalone pieces and refined over several years before being assembled into the final cut. This approach allowed the mythology to expand organically and gave the story room to evolve as new layers were added.

Because of the film’s heavy reliance on interviews and extensive archival-style news footage, the cast grew to nearly three times the size of a typical independent production. Journalists, investigators, witnesses, law enforcement figures, and community members all required distinct voices and believable histories. Each performer had to feel like a real person who existed long before the camera arrived. The cumulative effect creates a world that feels lived-in and disturbingly plausible rather than staged.

Made by a small, self-funded team, nearly every dollar went directly onto the screen. We built an internal lore archive filled with timelines, articles, and backstories that extend far beyond what audiences see. Locations were chosen for authenticity over scale. Performances were calibrated to feel natural and unpolished. Even Mr. Shiny’s mask evolved through numerous iterations before landing on its deceptively simple, unsettling final form. The finished film may feel unearthed, but behind it is years of careful assembly, collaboration, and an obsessive commitment to detail.
